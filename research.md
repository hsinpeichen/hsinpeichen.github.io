<h2 class="wp-block-heading">Master's Thesis: Exploring the Observability of Surviving Companions of Stripped-envelope Supernovae</h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:media-text {"mediaId":275,"mediaLink":"https://hsinpeichen.com/research/stsci-01fzt6z6hfzjc4cxa33ajrvc78-2/","mediaType":"image"} -->

<!-- /wp:paragraph --></div></div>
<!-- /wp:media-text -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>In 2020, I began my Master’s studies at NTHU, under the guidance of <a href="https://kuochuanpan.github.io/ceag/" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener">Dr. Kuo-Chuan Pan</a>. My Master's thesis focused on simulating the surviving companions of stripped-envelope supernovae (SNe). Through hydrodynamics simulations of the SN impact on the companion and the evolutionary paths of the surviving companion, we uncovered that the surviving companion becomes temporarily brighter and redder, enhancing the chances of detection. By applying observational constraints from SN 2020oi, we further predicted that the possible surviving companion could be observed by Hubble Space Telescope (HST) and James Webb Space Telescope (JWST) 10 years after the explosion. This project was published in the <em>Astrophysical Journal</em> (<a href="https://ui.adsabs.harvard.edu/abs/2023arXiv230402662C/abstract" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener">Chen, Rau, &amp; Pan, 2023</a>) and featured in the <a href="https://aasnova.org/2023/07/19/wanted-surviving-companions-of-stripped-envelope-supernovae/" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener">AAS<em> </em>Nova</a><em> </em>website in July 2023.</p>

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>After graduating in February 2023, I continued working as a Research Assistant at the <a href="https://phys.ncts.ntu.edu.tw/" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener">National Center for Theoretical Science</a> in Taiwan under the mentorship of Professor Pan. I embarked on my next project, exploring the systematic study of surviving companions in the symbiotic channel of type Ia supernovae. Our research revealed that the evolved companions could maintain their luminosity from before the SN explosion or transform into hot, bright dwarves. We have submitted the paper to <em>ApJ</em>, and it is currently under revision. Through these projects of simulations of supernovae, I have received solid training in conducting simulations using stellar evolution code <a href="https://docs.mesastar.org/en/latest/index.html" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener">MESA</a> and hydrodynamics simulation code <a href="https://flash.rochester.edu/site/index.shtml" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener">FLASH</a>, and also have enhanced my abilities in problem solving, literature review, and scientific writing.</p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">2nd-Year Project at UT: Synthetic Observations of Cosmic Ray Tracers with STARFORGE Simulations</h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:media-text {"mediaId":286,"mediaLink":"https://hsinpeichen.com/research/starforge-simulation-snapshot-scaled/","mediaType":"image"} -->
<div class="wp-block-media-text is-stacked-on-mobile"><figure class="wp-block-media-text__media"><img src="https://hsinpeichen.wordpress.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/05/starforge-simulation-snapshot-scaled-1.png?w=1024" alt="" class="wp-image-286 size-full" /></figure><div class="wp-block-media-text__content"><!-- wp:paragraph {"placeholder":"Content…"} -->
<p><em>A snapshot of STARFORGE simulation. Image Credit: Northwestern University/UT Austin</em></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph --></div></div>
<!-- /wp:media-text -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Now, as a member of <a href="https://sites.google.com/view/stellaoffner/" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener">Dr. Stella Offner</a>'s research group at UT Austin, my first two years of graduate study are dedicated to investigating the effects of cosmic rays in star-forming regions. Cosmic rays are a significant factor influencing various aspects of star formation, including chemical abundances, gas temperature, and star formation efficiencies. In this project, I will use numerical simulation data from STARFORGE (<a href="https://ui.adsabs.harvard.edu/abs/2024ApJ...973...16F/abstract" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener">Fitz Axen et al. 2024</a>) to examine how cosmic rays affect chemistry of star-forming clouds. My goal is to produce synthetic observations of cosmic ray tracers and to evaluate the accuracy of the CRIRs derived in molecular cloud observations.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->
